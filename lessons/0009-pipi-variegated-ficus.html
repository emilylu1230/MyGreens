About 皮皮

皮皮 is a variegated cultivar of Ficus elastica with bronze-green mixed variegation — patches of pale cream, bronze, and green on the same leaf. The care is identical to 刚刚 Ganggang, with one important difference: variegated plants need more light to maintain their patterns.

Why variegated plants need more light

Variegation means portions of the leaf lack chlorophyll — those pale cream or bronze patches can't photosynthesise. The plant has to do all its light-work through the green sections only. This means a variegated plant works harder per leaf and needs a brighter environment to compensate.

In low light, two things happen: growth slows dramatically, and new leaves come in with less variegation — they revert toward solid green as the plant prioritises survival over aesthetics. This is irreversible in the affected leaves.

Give 皮皮 the best spot Of your three Ficus plants (Ganggang, 皮皮, and the Ficus cutting), 皮皮 should get the brightest indirect spot. Near the window, behind a sheer curtain — not further back in the room.

Light

Bright indirect light, as close to the window as possible without direct sun hitting the leaves. If your window is north-facing, a spot 0.5–1 metre from the glass behind a sheer curtain is ideal. East-facing works well too.

Watch for: new leaves coming in more green than the parent — this means not enough light. Move it closer to the window.

Watering

Identical to Ganggang: let the top 3–4 cm dry out before watering. Water thoroughly, then let it drain completely. In winter, reduce to every 2–3 weeks.

The latex sap is the same — wash hands after handling, keep away from pets.

Keeping leaves clean

The large, waxy leaves accumulate dust, which reduces light absorption. Wipe leaves monthly with a damp soft cloth. This is particularly important for 皮皮 since variegated leaves already photosynthesize less efficiently.

Common problems

New leaves losing variegation (more green)
Not enough light — the most important sign to watch for.
Move to a brighter indirect spot immediately.
Yellow leaves
Overwatering, especially in winter.
Reduce watering frequency. Ensure pot has drainage.
Leaf drop
Being moved, drafts, or temperature change — Ficus hates disruption.
Find a stable spot and leave it. Avoid cold drafts from open windows in winter.
Brown scorched patches
Direct sun on the pale variegated sections, which have no UV protection.
Move away from direct sun. Variegated patches burn faster than green ones.

Seasonal care — Adelaide

  • Winter (now): Reduce watering. Maintain bright spot — variegation suffers most in winter low light. No fertiliser.
  • Spring: Resume normal watering and fertilising. Clean leaves before growth surge.
  • Summer: Protect pale sections from direct sun — they scorch faster than on all-green plants.
