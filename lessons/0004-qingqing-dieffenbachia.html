Toxicity — important Dieffenbachia sap causes temporary numbness and burning if it touches your mouth or eyes. Keep away from children and pets. Always wash hands after handling. The common name "Dumb Cane" comes from this effect — it temporarily affects speech if ingested.

About Qingqing

Dieffenbachia 'Sterling' is a variegated variety with large, paddle-shaped leaves — typically cream or pale green centres with darker green edges. It's a robust, fast-growing tropical that tolerates lower light better than most variegated plants, which makes it versatile in your bedroom.

Light

Qingqing is comfortable in medium indirect light — a few metres from your window, or near the window but behind a thick curtain. It will also tolerate lower light, but growth slows significantly and the pale variegation may become less pronounced.

Avoid direct sun entirely — it scorches the pale sections of the leaves quickly because those areas have less protective chlorophyll.

Watering

Let the top 3 cm dry out before watering. Qingqing is moderately drought-tolerant — a missed watering won't hurt it, but sitting in wet soil will cause root rot.

Fluoride sensitivity Dieffenbachia is sensitive to fluoride in tap water — brown leaf tips are often a sign of this, not low humidity. If you notice tips browning, switch to filtered water or leave tap water to sit overnight before using (chlorine evaporates; fluoride doesn't, so a filter is better).

Common problems

Brown leaf tips
Fluoride in tap water, or low humidity.
Use filtered water. Keep away from heating and air conditioning vents.
Yellow lower leaves
Natural ageing (lowest leaves yellow as plant grows) — or overwatering if multiple leaves at once.
One or two lower leaves yellowing is normal. Multiple yellowing = check soil moisture.
Drooping, soft stems
Overwatering leading to root rot, or severe underwatering.
Check soil — if wet and smelly, remove from pot, trim black roots, repot in fresh mix. If bone dry, water thoroughly.
Pale, washed-out leaves
Too much direct light bleaching the variegation.
Move further from window or add a thicker curtain layer.

Seasonal care — Adelaide

  • Winter (now): Reduce watering. Keep above 15°C — cold causes stem rot. Don't place near a cold window overnight.
  • Spring/Summer: Growth speeds up. Can stay indoors year-round — not suitable for outdoor Adelaide heat.
