About 棠棠

Begonia maculata — the polka dot begonia — is one of the most visually striking houseplants. The white spots on the olive-green leaves are a natural feature called trichomes, not damage or disease. The undersides of the leaves are deep red-purple. In good conditions it produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers.

棠棠 is moderately demanding — more so than a rubber tree, but easier than the Calatheas. The main challenges are light, watering balance, and air circulation.

Light

Begonia maculata needs bright indirect light to thrive. It does well near a window behind a sheer curtain. East-facing light (gentle morning sun) is ideal — it provides enough intensity without the harsh afternoon heat that would burn the leaves.

In low light: the plant becomes leggy, loses its compact shape, and may stop flowering. In direct hot sun: leaves scorch and spots fade.

Watering

Let the top 3 cm dry out before watering, then water thoroughly. Begonias are susceptible to root rot if kept too wet, but also drop leaves if they dry out too much. The balance matters more here than with your more forgiving plants.

Water at soil level — getting water on the leaves can cause spots and fungal issues, especially in cooler weather.

Don't let it sit in water Empty the saucer after watering. Begonias develop root rot quickly when roots are waterlogged. This is the most common way they're killed.

Air circulation

Good airflow is important for Begonia maculata — stagnant humid air encourages powdery mildew, a white dusty fungus on the leaves. Your openable window is perfect for this — occasional gentle ventilation (not cold drafts) keeps the air moving without stressing the plant.

Flowering

With enough bright indirect light and consistent care, 棠棠 produces dangling clusters of small white flowers with yellow centres. To encourage flowering:

  • Bright indirect light — this is the main trigger
  • Fertilise monthly in spring/summer with a balanced or slightly phosphorus-heavy fertiliser
  • Don't repot unnecessarily — slightly pot-bound plants flower more readily

Common problems

Powdery white coating on leaves
Powdery mildew — fungal, caused by poor air circulation + humidity.
Improve airflow. Remove affected leaves. Wipe remaining leaves with diluted neem oil. Don't mist.
Leggy, sparse growth
Not enough light.
Move to brighter indirect spot. Pinch growing tips to encourage bushier growth.
Leaf drop
Overwatering, cold drafts, or being moved.
Let soil dry more. Keep away from cold windows overnight. Find a stable spot and leave it there.
Brown spots on leaves
Water splashed on leaves, or sunburn.
Water at soil level. Move slightly away from direct light if near window.

Seasonal care — Adelaide

  • Winter (now): Reduce watering. No fertiliser. Ensure good airflow but no cold drafts. Growth slows.
  • Spring: Prune leggy stems by half to encourage bushy regrowth. Resume fertilising.
  • Summer: Keep indoors in bright indirect light. Don't move outside — too harsh.
