Light

Ganggang needs bright indirect light to maintain its deep burgundy colour. In lower light the leaves gradually revert toward green — this is the plant trying to maximise chlorophyll to compensate for less light.

In your master bedroom: place near the window but behind a sheer curtain. Avoid direct afternoon sun, which scorches the leaves even in winter.

Adelaide summer On days above 38°C, move Ganggang away from the window entirely. Glass amplifies heat and the large leaves are easily scorched. Keep curtains drawn on extreme heat days.

Watering

Let the top 3–4 cm of soil dry out before watering again. Push your finger into the soil — if it comes out with moist soil clinging to it, wait. If it comes out dry and clean, water thoroughly until it drains from the bottom.

In Adelaide winter (June–August): reduce to once every 2–3 weeks. The plant grows very slowly in winter and sitting in wet soil causes root rot. This is especially important now while the cracked branch is healing — dry conditions help the wound callous over.

Milky sap warning Ficus elastica produces a white latex sap when leaves or stems are cut or damaged. This sap can irritate skin and is toxic to cats and dogs. Wipe up any sap with a damp cloth. Wear gloves when pruning.

The cracked branch — what to do

  1. Now: Support the cracked branch with a soft tie or cloth strip — not tight, just stable. Don't try to seal the crack with anything.
  2. Keep soil drier than usual — less water means less stress on the wound.
  3. No fertiliser until September (Adelaide winter rule).
  4. August assessment: If the crack has calloused over (dried, brown edge), the branch is stable. At that point you can consider a clean top-cut just above a node to encourage two new branches from below.
  5. If the branch continues to split or droops severely, remove it cleanly with a sterilised blade.

Common problems

Yellow leaves
Almost always overwatering, especially in winter.
Let soil dry out more between waterings. Check that the pot has drainage holes.
Brown, crispy leaf edges
Low humidity, cold drafts, or tap water fluoride.
Move away from air conditioning. Try using filtered or rainwater.
Leaves dropping suddenly
Temperature shock or being moved. Ficus hates change.
Once you find a good spot, leave it there. Avoid drafts from open windows in winter.
Colour fading to green
Not enough light. Dark leaves need good light to stay dark.
Move closer to the window (still behind sheer curtain).
Sticky residue on leaves
Scale insects or mealybugs.
Wipe leaves with a cloth dampened with diluted neem oil or a drop of dish soap in water. Check under leaves and along stems.

Seasonal care — Adelaide

  • Winter (now – Aug): Reduce watering. No fertiliser. Keep above 12°C — no cold windowsills overnight.
  • Spring (Sept): Resume normal watering. Apply slow-release fertiliser once soil temp is above 18°C.
  • Summer (Nov–Mar): Protect from direct sun on hot days. Can move outside to a shaded spot if needed.
